Project 2: Rainfall-Runoff and Flood Simulation

Urban hydrology modeling and 2D flood simulation using SWAT and TUFLOW

Project Overview
Technologies Used QSWAT+, MapSWAT, Google Earth Engine (GEE), TUFLOW, DEM, LULC, Soil Data

This project aimed to evaluate the hydrological behavior and flood vulnerability of town planning. We performed a comprehensive rainfall-runoff analysis using the SWAT (Soil and Water Assessment Tool) model, which simulates the impact of land use, soil properties, topography, and climate on watershed hydrology.

To begin, we delineated the watershed and created Hydrological Response Units (HRUs) using input datasets such as soil maps, land use/land cover (LULC), and Digital Elevation Models (DEM). These datasets were accessed through MapSWAT, a tool that utilizes Google Earth Engine (GEE) to gather spatially accurate environmental data.

Once the watershed was configured in SWAT, we simulated rainfall-runoff patterns to understand how water moves across the landscape during storm events. The model helped estimate surface runoff, infiltration, and flow accumulation, giving insight into how different sub-basins contribute to the total runoff.

To assess flood risks and visualize potential flood spread, we used TUFLOW, a 2D hydraulic modeling software. By inputting runoff data from SWAT into TUFLOW, we simulated how water would propagate over the terrain during heavy rainfall events. This helped identify low-lying and flood-prone zones, providing a visual representation of flood impact areas.

The combination of SWAT and TUFLOW models enabled us to create a realistic and spatially accurate assessment of flood risks. The results of this study offer valuable guidance for urban development, infrastructure planning, and disaster management strategies.
